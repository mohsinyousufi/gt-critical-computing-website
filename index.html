Critical Computing Research Group

Examining the social, political, and ethical dimensions of computing technologies at Georgia Institute of Technology

Critical Computing Perspectives

We investigate how computing technologies intersect with power, justice, and social change. Our interdisciplinary approach combines computer science with science and technology studies, critical theory, and community engagement.

Community-Centered Research

Our research is grounded in collaboration with communities most impacted by technological systems. We prioritize participatory methods and community ownership of research outcomes.

Research Areas

Algorithmic Justice

Investigating bias, fairness, and accountability in automated decision-making systems, with focus on criminal justice, hiring, and social services.

Platform Governance

Analyzing how digital platforms shape public discourse, democratic participation, and social movements through content moderation and algorithmic curation.

Data Justice

Examining power dynamics in data collection, ownership, and use, with emphasis on surveillance, privacy, and community data sovereignty.

AI Ethics in Practice

Moving beyond abstract principles to understand how ethical considerations are implemented (or ignored) in real-world AI development and deployment.

Digital Labor

Studying the changing nature of work in the digital economy, including gig work, content moderation, and AI training labor.

Technology Policy

Analyzing policy interventions in technology governance, from local ordinances to international regulatory frameworks.
