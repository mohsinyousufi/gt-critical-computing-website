Regular Programming

Weekly Working Group

When: Every Friday, 2-4 PM
Where: Klaus 2456
A regular meeting space for group members to share ongoing research, discuss readings, and collaborate on projects. Open to all group members and invited guests.

Monthly Book Club

When: First Thursday of each month, 6-7:30 PM
Where: Rotating locations
Open to the wider Georgia Tech community, we read and discuss foundational texts in critical computing and STS.

Quarterly Symposiums

When: September, December, March, June
Where: Georgia Tech campus
Large-scale events bringing together researchers from across the Southeast to engage with current issues at the intersection of computing and society.

Community Guidelines

Our events prioritize inclusive, anti-oppressive practices. Read our community guidelines to learn about our approach to creating brave spaces for difficult conversations about technology and justice.

Get Involved

Our events are open to Georgia Tech students, faculty, staff, and community members from the broader Atlanta area. We especially welcome participation from folks whose communities are most impacted by technological systems.

Stay Updated: Subscribe to our mailing list for event announcements and updates. Email criticalcomputing@gatech.edu with "Subscribe" in the subject line.

Propose an Event: Have an idea for a workshop, panel, or discussion? We welcome community-initiated programming that aligns with our critical computing focus.

Accessibility: All our events are designed to be accessible. If you have specific accessibility needs or want to help make our events more inclusive, please reach out.
