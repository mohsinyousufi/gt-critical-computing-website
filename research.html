Research & Projects

Investigating the social, political, and ethical dimensions of computing through community-engaged scholarship

Current Research Projects

Algorithmic Bias in Municipal Systems

Funding: NSF Award #2025-1234 ($1.2M)
Duration: 2025-2028
PI: Dr. Aria Chen

Examining how algorithmic decision-making systems in city government perpetuate and amplify existing inequalities. Partnership with Atlanta city government to study bias in housing assistance, permitting, and social services allocation.

Community Data Governance in the South

Funding: Ford Foundation ($250K)
Duration: 2024-2026
PI: Dr. Samara Patel

Participatory research with three Atlanta neighborhoods developing community-controlled approaches to data collection, ownership, and use in smart city initiatives.

Platform Labor and Organizing

Funding: Open Society Foundations ($180K)
Duration: 2025-2027
PI: Dr. Luis Martinez

Studying how gig workers in Atlanta use digital tools and platforms to organize for better working conditions, in collaboration with local labor organizations.

AI Ethics in Practice

Funding: Mozilla Foundation ($120K)
Duration: 2024-2025
PI: Dr. David Biggs

Ethnographic study of how AI ethics principles are (or aren't) implemented in actual technology development processes at companies and startups.

Research Areas

Algorithmic Justice

Moving beyond "fairness" metrics to examine how automated systems reproduce and challenge existing power structures. Focus areas include criminal justice algorithms, hiring systems, and social service allocation.

Platform Governance

Analyzing how digital platforms shape public discourse, democratic participation, and social movements through content moderation, algorithmic curation, and platform policies.

Data Justice

Examining power dynamics in data collection, ownership, and use. Research includes surveillance capitalism, privacy, and community data sovereignty movements.

Digital Labor

Studying the changing nature of work in the digital economy, including gig work, content moderation, AI training labor, and platform-mediated organizing.

Technology Policy

Analyzing policy interventions in technology governance, from local ordinances to international regulatory frameworks, with attention to community impact and participation.

Decolonizing Computing

Exploring alternative approaches to computing education, research methods, and technology design that center Indigenous and Global South knowledge systems.

Research Methods & Approach

Community-Engaged Research

Our research is grounded in long-term partnerships with communities most impacted by technological systems. We prioritize participatory methods, community ownership of research outcomes, and accountable scholarship practices.

Interdisciplinary Collaboration

Critical computing requires perspectives from computer science, STS, media studies, public policy, sociology, and beyond. Our group brings together diverse methodological approaches and theoretical frameworks.

Anti-Oppressive Research Practices

We are committed to research practices that challenge rather than reproduce systems of oppression. This includes attention to power dynamics in research relationships, community consent processes, and equitable benefit-sharing.

Open Science & Community Access

Research findings, datasets (where appropriate), and tools are shared openly to benefit community organizations and other researchers. We publish in open-access venues and develop community-accessible resources.

Tools & Resources

Community Algorithm Auditing Toolkit

Open-source tools and methods for community organizations to assess algorithmic systems that impact their communities. Developed through our municipal algorithms project.

Platform Worker Organizing Guide

Practical resource for gig workers and labor organizers, documenting digital tools and strategies for collective action in platform-mediated work.

Critical Computing Curriculum

Teaching materials for integrating critical perspectives into computer science education, including syllabi, assignments, and pedagogical approaches.

Collaborate With Us

We welcome collaboration with researchers, community organizations, and practitioners working at the intersection of technology and social justice.

For Community Organizations: If you're working on technology justice issues and interested in research collaboration, we'd love to explore partnership opportunities that center community needs and ownership.

For Researchers: We're open to collaborative projects, co-authored papers, and shared events with scholars working on critical computing topics across disciplines.

For Students: We offer research opportunities for undergraduate and graduate students from diverse academic backgrounds. See our People page for current group members.
